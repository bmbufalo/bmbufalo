</h3>

<div class="post-body entry-content">
<div align="justify">
<div class='p_embed p_image_embed'>
<img alt="Media_http4bpblogspot_ebcjh" height="296" src="https://brian.bufalo.me/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/media_http4bpblogspot_ebcjh-scaled600.jpg?w=300" width="400" />
</div>
 The Earth and Moon were <strong><a href="http://www.psi.edu/projects/moon/moon.html">created</a></strong> as the result of a giant <strong><a href="http://spaceports.blogspot.com/2007/11/hartmann-theory-put-to-test.html">collision between two planets</a></strong> the size of Mars and Venus. However, new research from the <a href="http://www.nbi.ku.dk/english/news/news10/the_earth_and_moon_formed_later_than_previously_thought/"><strong>Niels Bohr Institute</strong> </a>shows that the Earth and Moon must have formed much later - perhaps up to <strong>150 million years</strong> after the formation of the solar system. The research results have been published in the scientific journal, <strong><a href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science?_ob=ArticleURL&amp;_udi=B6V61-5021JBF-1&amp;_user=10&amp;_coverDate=06%2F15%2F2010&amp;_rdoc=1&amp;_fmt=high&amp;_orig=search&amp;_sort=d&amp;_docanchor=&amp;view=c&amp;_acct=C000050221&amp;_version=1&amp;_urlVersion=0&amp;_userid=10&amp;md5=e15153fdc85a477e5808941d9f22b22e">Earth and Planetary Science Letters</a></strong>, according to a report in <strong><a href="http://www.moondaily.com/reports/The_Earth_And_Moon_Formed_Later_Than_Previously_Thought_999.html">Moon Daily</a></strong>.</div>
